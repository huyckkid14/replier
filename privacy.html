Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 25, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Replier collects, uses, discloses, retains, and protects information when you use Replier to assist with Gmail replies. By using Replier, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with this Privacy Policy, do not use Replier.

1. Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to information processed by Replier in connection with Gmail reply drafting, review, draft creation, and email sending features. It does not apply to services, websites, platforms, or products operated by third parties, including Google, Gmail, Cloudflare, OpenAI, your browser provider, email recipients, or any network or device provider. Those third parties process information under their own policies and terms.

2. Information We Process

Replier may process the following categories of information:

  1. Gmail message data. This may include message IDs, thread IDs, sender and recipient information, subject lines, message headers, message body content, snippets, unread inbox status, and related metadata needed to identify messages, create drafts, or send replies.
  2. Account authorization data. Replier uses Google OAuth to obtain authorization for Gmail API access. OAuth credentials are used to operate authorized Gmail features and are handled in accordance with applicable Google platform requirements.
  3. User settings. Replier may store reply mode, saved template selections, custom template text, tone preference, trusted-sender settings, trusted-sender lists, reset/check history settings, and the user's name if provided.
  4. Generated content. Replier may generate drafts, template replies, review results, error messages, block notices, diagnostic sections, and disclosure text stating that an email was automatically generated by Replier.
  5. Processing history. Replier may store processed Gmail message IDs to reduce repeated handling of the same unread messages.
  6. Technical information. Replier may process operational information such as authorization status, API response status, runtime events, extension errors, and other diagnostic information needed to provide, secure, and troubleshoot the service.

3. Gmail Permissions

Replier requests Gmail permissions that may allow it to read Gmail messages, compose drafts, and send Gmail messages. These permissions are used only to provide user-facing reply functionality, including locating unread inbox messages, extracting message content, generating replies, creating drafts, and sending replies according to user-selected settings.

Replier does not use Gmail data for advertising. Replier does not sell Gmail data. Replier does not use Gmail data to determine creditworthiness or for unrelated profiling. Replier's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements, where applicable.

4. How We Use Information

Replier may use information to:

  1. provide Gmail reply drafting, review, draft creation, and sending functionality;
  2. apply user-selected modes, templates, tone settings, trusted-sender settings, and name/signature preferences;
  3. generate AI-assisted draft replies and automated review decisions;
  4. create draft-only fallback messages when review fails, a sender is not trusted for auto-send, an error occurs, or the original message indicates that no reply should be sent;
  5. avoid repeatedly processing the same unread message;
  6. include diagnostic information in generated drafts or messages when needed to explain automated processing; and
  7. maintain, test, secure, and improve Replier.

5. AI Processing

Replier may transmit email content and related context to a Cloudflare Worker backend for routing to OpenAI services for drafting and review. Information sent for AI processing may include sender information, subject lines, message body text, proposed draft replies, user settings relevant to drafting or review, and review instructions. In review modes, Replier may submit the same original message and draft multiple times for independent review.

Cloudflare provides backend infrastructure used to route AI-processing requests. Cloudflare processes information according to its applicable terms and privacy policy, available at https://www.cloudflare.com/privacypolicy/. OpenAI processes information according to its own applicable terms, privacy commitments, and API data policies. You should not use Replier with information you are unwilling to have processed by Cloudflare, OpenAI, and the systems necessary to transmit that information.

6. Drafting, Sending, and Automated Decisions

Replier can be configured to create drafts or send replies automatically. Automated output may be inaccurate, incomplete, inappropriate, or inconsistent with your intent, tone, obligations, or preferences. You are responsible for choosing settings carefully and reviewing drafts when appropriate.

Replier may block sending and create a draft instead when a review fails, when a sender is not in a trusted list, when the original message appears to request no response, when the generated draft contains placeholders, or when an error occurs. These safeguards reduce risk but do not guarantee that every issue will be detected.

7. Diagnostic Information and Recipient Disclosure

Replier may include diagnostic information in generated drafts or sent emails below a marker such as "-- IGNORE TEXT BELOW THIS LINE --". Diagnostic information may include original email text extracted by Replier, generated draft text, review results, raw reviewer responses, error details, block reasons, and automation status.

Replier may also include the disclosure: "This email was automatically generated by Replier." When present, this disclosure is intended to inform recipients that the message was automatically generated.

If you do not want diagnostic information or automated-generation disclosures included in outbound messages, do not enable automated sending unless your settings and version of Replier are configured accordingly.

8. Storage and Retention

Replier may store user settings using browser-provided storage and may store processed message IDs using local browser storage. Some settings may sync across browsers or devices depending on the user's browser account and synchronization settings.

Settings generally remain until changed, cleared, or removed by the user, the browser, or Replier. Processed message IDs remain until reset, cleared, overwritten, or removed. Information transmitted to Google, Cloudflare, OpenAI, email recipients, or other third parties is subject to those parties' retention practices.

9. Sharing and Disclosure

Replier may disclose or transmit information:

  1. to Google and Gmail, as necessary to authenticate access, read messages, create drafts, and send emails;
  2. to Cloudflare and OpenAI, as necessary to route requests and generate or review email content;
  3. to email recipients, when Replier creates or sends messages containing generated content, diagnostic information, disclosures, or other included text;
  4. to browser storage systems, as necessary to store settings and processing history;
  5. to service providers or infrastructure providers if future versions use hosted services; and
  6. when required by law, legal process, security needs, fraud prevention, enforcement of rights, or protection of users or others.

Replier does not sell personal information or Gmail message content.

10. Security

Replier uses OAuth authorization and communicates with Gmail, Cloudflare, and OpenAI over HTTPS. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure. Users should not use Replier for highly sensitive, confidential, regulated, privileged, emergency, financial, medical, legal, or educational-record communications unless they have independently determined that the service is appropriate for that use.

Replier uses technical and organizational safeguards designed to protect service credentials and user information.

11. Sensitive Information

Replier is not designed for highly sensitive information, including passwords, government identifiers, financial account information, health information, legal matters, emergency communications, confidential school records, or similarly sensitive data. Automated review is not a substitute for human judgment.

12. User Controls

Users may control Replier by changing reply modes, editing templates, updating trusted senders, disabling trusted-only auto-send, resetting checked emails, disconnecting Google access, clearing stored Replier data, disabling Replier, uninstalling Replier, or revoking Google OAuth access through Google Account permissions.

13. Data Deletion

You may delete locally stored settings and processing history by using available reset controls, clearing Replier's stored data, disabling or uninstalling Replier, or revoking Google access through your Google Account. Data already transmitted to Google, Cloudflare, OpenAI, or email recipients may be governed by those parties' policies and may not be removable by Replier.

14. Children

Replier is not directed to children under 13. If Replier is used by minors, students, schools, or educational organizations, the user or deploying organization is responsible for compliance with applicable laws, consent requirements, school policies, and data protection obligations.

15. International Processing

Information may be processed in the United States and other jurisdictions where Google, Cloudflare, OpenAI, infrastructure providers, or relevant service providers operate. By using Replier, you understand that information may be processed outside your state, province, or country of residence.

16. Privacy Rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, restrict, object to, or obtain a copy of certain personal information. You may also have the right to withdraw consent where processing is based on consent. Requests may be submitted through the support contact designated for Replier.

Replier does not sell personal information. Replier does not use Gmail data for advertising.

17. No Professional Advice

Replier does not provide legal, medical, financial, educational, employment, emergency, or other professional advice. Generated messages should not be treated as professional advice or as a substitute for your own judgment.

18. Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time. The "Last Updated" date reflects the latest revision. Continued use of Replier after an update means you acknowledge the updated Privacy Policy.
